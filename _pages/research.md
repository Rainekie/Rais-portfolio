My work connects physical acoustics, real-time systems, and human perception. The projects below span acoustic capture, modeling, rendering, and evaluation.

<section class="research-section" id="aar" data-index="01">
  <p class="research-meta">2020—Present · Auditory augmented reality</p>
  <h2>Real-time virtual acoustics for AAR</h2>
  <img src="{{ '/assets/img/research-arae.jpg' | relative_url }}" alt="ARAE interface, room-scale test setup, and a reconstructed 3D room model">
  <p>A room-aware rendering framework that makes mobile virtual sound adapt to the listener's immediate physical surroundings. The system combines object-based game audio, real-time 3D environment analysis, and dynamic room-acoustic modeling under the latency and compute constraints of mobile auditory augmented reality.</p>
  <p class="research-tags">Room acoustics · LiDAR · Real-time DSP · 6DoF audio</p>
  <p class="selected-output"><strong>Selected output:</strong> “An approach to real-time late reverberation generation for six-degrees-of-freedom Auditory Augmented Reality,” 189th Meeting of the Acoustical Society of America, 2025. Invited talk.</p>
</section>

<section class="research-section" id="heritage" data-index="02">
  <p class="research-meta">2023—Present · Aural heritage</p>
  <h2>Explorable soundscapes for cultural memory</h2>
  <img src="{{ '/assets/img/research-heritage.jpg' | relative_url }}" alt="Ambisonic recording at Magoksa Temple, a virtual reconstruction, and a 6DoF listening setup">
  <p>I developed an end-to-end workflow from on-site high-order ambisonic capture to post-processing and real-time rendering in VR. The resulting 6DoF environments allow listeners to move through reconstructed heritage spaces with spatial realism and presence.</p>
  <p class="research-tags">Ambisonics · VR · Field recording · Heritage</p>
  <p class="selected-output"><strong>Selected output:</strong> “Virtual Reconstruction of Historical Heritage: A 6DoF Immersive Audio-Visual Reproduction of Magoksa Temple,” IEEE I3DA, 2025.</p>
</section>

<section class="research-section" id="binaural" data-index="03">
  <p class="research-meta">2023—Present · Perceptual evaluation</p>
  <h2>What makes binaural audio feel right?</h2>
  <img src="{{ '/assets/img/research-binaural.jpg' | relative_url }}" alt="Participants evaluating head-tracked binaural renderers in two listening rooms">
  <p>Through controlled listening tests and statistical analysis, I study how spatial accuracy, timbre, and externalization shape preference for head-tracked binaural renderers. The work provides creators and developers with an evidence-based framework for balancing competing acoustic attributes.</p>
  <p class="research-tags">Psychoacoustics · Binaural audio · Statistics · Head tracking</p>
  <p class="selected-output"><strong>Latest article:</strong> “Regional differences in listener preferences for head-tracked binaural audio,” <em>npj Acoustics</em>, 2, 23, 2026.</p>
</section>

<section class="research-section" id="automotive" data-index="04">
  <p class="research-meta">2024—Present · Automotive acoustics</p>
  <h2>Adaptive sound fields for next-generation vehicles</h2>
  <p>A joint research project with Hyundai Motor Company exploring context-aware acoustic control for Purpose-Built Vehicles. As project leader, I designed a Max/MSP-based real-time processing framework and multichannel array for active reverberation with acoustic-feedback mitigation.</p>
  <p class="research-tags">Active acoustics · Max/MSP · Multichannel audio · PBV</p>
</section>
